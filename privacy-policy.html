Privacy Policy — Signal Lost

Last updated: July 2026

Signal Lost ("the App") is an educational game that teaches HTML through an interactive story. This policy explains what information the App collects and how it's used.

What we collect

The App stores your mission progress (which lessons and challenges you've completed) so you can pick up where you left off. To do this, the App generates a random, anonymous player ID on your device — this ID is not linked to your name, email, or any other personal information, and you are never asked to create an account or log in.

How it's stored

Progress data is saved using Google Firebase (Cloud Firestore), a third-party service. Firebase's own privacy practices are described at firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

What we don't collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any contact information.
  • We do not display advertisements.
  • We do not use analytics or tracking tools beyond the progress-saving described above.
  • We do not share, sell, or otherwise distribute any data to third parties.

Children's privacy

The App does not knowingly collect any personal information from anyone, including children. Since no personal information is collected at all, there is nothing to identify or contact any user, regardless of age.

Your data

Because progress is tied only to an anonymous device-generated ID, there is no way for us to identify which data belongs to a specific person. If you'd like your progress data removed, you can use the in-app "Reset Progress" option, which permanently deletes your saved progress.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this same page with a revised "Last updated" date.
